Some derivatives are very complicated to evaluate using the methods from [[Derivatives]] and [[L'Hopital's Rule]] it is better to just memorize their answers.
### Common Trig 
1. $$\frac{d}{dx} sin(x) = cos(x)$$
2. $$\frac{d}{dx}cos(x) = -sin(x)$$
3. $$\frac{d}{dx} tan(x) = sec^2(x)$$
4. $$\frac{d}{dx} sec(x) = sec(x)\cdot tan(x)$$
5. $$\frac{d}{dx}csc(x) = -csc(x)\cdot cot(x)$$
6. $$\frac{d}{dx}cot(x) = -csc^2(x)$$
### Inverse Trig 
1. $$\frac{d}{dx}\arcsin(x) = \frac{1}{\sqrt{1-x^2}}$$
2. $$\frac{d}{dx}\arccos(x) = -\frac{1}{\sqrt{1-x^2}}$$
3. $$\frac{d}{dx}\arctan(x) = \frac{1}{1+x^2}$$
### Logarithms and exponentials
1. $$\frac{d}{dx}a^x=a^x \cdot ln(a)$$
2. $$\frac{d}{dx}e^x = e^x$$
3. $$\frac{d}{dx}ln(x) = \frac{1}{x},\ x>0$$
4. $$\frac{d}{dx}ln|x| = \frac{1}{x},\ x\neq 0$$
5. $$\frac{d}{dx}(log_a(x)) = \frac{1}{x\,ln(a)},\ x>0$$
