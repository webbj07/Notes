L'Hopital's Rule is used to evaluate quotient limits that result in indeterminate forms such as $\frac{0}{0}$ or $\frac{\infty}{\infty}$.

L'Hopital's Rule states that if a quotient limit is indeterminate, $$\lim \frac{f(x)}{g(x)} = \lim \frac{f^\prime(x)}{g^\prime(x)}$$ This means that derivative of the numerator and denominator can be taken in order to get the limit into a determinate form or a form that can be evaluated using [[Continuity]].
